The Early American Foreign Service Database

The EAFSD places biographical and professional information about all foreign service officers in a relational data structure. This data structure allows users to trace the early American governments' attempts to deploy and control their overseas representatives.

The database also recreates the correspondence networks that sprang up between the officers as they sought from each other the information and expertise necessary to fulfill their duties.

The system is also my dissertation. It holds essays and data visualizations which express my arguments about the nature of the Early American Foreign Service. However, the argument goes much deeper than the words and images present on the EXPLORE and VISUAL ESSAYS pages.

The data model is an argument on the nature of diplomacy and diplomatic history. I have included a methdological essay which you can read here (link will go here as soon as the essay is done),

What is Project Quincy?

Project Quincy is a Django application with a MySQL database that uses information about people, places, and organizations to trace how social networks and institutions develop over time and through space.

It is named in honor of John Quincy Adams (pictured on the right whilst gravely observing a camera).

Project Quincy is growing out of my work on the EAFSD, but the code has such a wide range of applications, that it I expect it will soon take on a life of its own.
